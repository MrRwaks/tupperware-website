TUPPERWARE

Reusable food-storage products known for airtight seals and the famous Tupperware Party sales model.

History

Earl Tupper developed durable polyethylene food-storage containers in the 1940s, introducing flexible, airtight seals that kept food fresher. In the 1950s, Brownie Wise popularized the "Tupperware Party," a home-based direct-sales model that became a powerful example of social selling and provided income opportunities for many women. Over the decades Tupperware became known both for practical kitchen solutions and for collectible vintage pieces; modern lines emphasize BPA-free materials and a wide range of kitchen products.

Care & Usage Tips

  • Vent lids before microwaving; avoid heating sealed containers.
  • Prefer hand washing to extend life; if dishwasher‑safe, use the top rack.
  • Avoid abrasive cleaners and very high heat to prevent warping or discoloration.
